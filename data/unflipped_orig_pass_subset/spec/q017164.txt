In digital circuit design, it is common to use power and ground supply signals to ensure that the circuit operates correctly. In this problem, you are tasked with designing a module that defines four supply signals: VPWR, VGND, VPB, and VNB. VPWR and VGND are 1-bit signals that represent the power and ground voltage supplies, respectively. VPB and VNB are also 1-bit signals that represent the power and ground voltage supplies for a separate power domain.

Your task is to implement a Verilog module that defines these four supply signals. The module should be named `supply_signals` and should contain the following inputs and outputs:

- `input wire clk`: a clock signal
- `output reg VPWR`: a 1-bit signal representing the power supply voltage
- `output reg VGND`: a 1-bit signal representing the ground supply voltage
- `output reg VPB`: a 1-bit signal representing the power supply voltage for the separate power domain
- `output reg VNB`: a 1-bit signal representing the ground supply voltage for the separate power domain

The `VPWR` and `VPB` signals should be set to 1, while the `VGND` and `VNB` signals should be set to 0. The signals should be updated on the rising edge of the clock signal.
